SIPLACE MeasuringFeeder X

Before components are picked up from a feeder and placed, their electrical value can be checked to ensure that the components in the feeder are as specified.

It is also possible to carry out the check at defined intervals, e.g. every 100th component, to ensure that there are no "fake components" on a tape reel.

It is possible to check the capacitance, resistance, on-state voltage/polarity (with diodes) and, if necessary, the inductance of a component.

The first component of a feeder is picked up, pressed onto a contact area by the placement head and tested.

The contact area is made up of 2 contact plates with a gap of 0.2 mm between them.

The components can be tested along this gap. Depending on the size of the components, the measurement can take place at different places along the contact area.

The components are to be tested:

  • every time the feeder is replaced,

  • after any potential change of batch: After a "torn foil" error, after any splicing operation or any change of waffle pack tray.

  • Verification period: In the programming system, you can specify the number of components after which the measurement is to be repeated.

  • Verification sequence: In the programming system, you can specify a sequence of intervals at which components are to be inspected.

Reference run:

There are two fiducials on each of the contact plates. These are measured using the board camera to determine the exact position of the contact plates.

This is necessary in order to allow the placement head to precisely position the component to be tested.

Size of components that can be measured using the SIPLACE MeasuringFeeder X:

0.6 mm x 0.3 mm through 6.0 mm x 3.2 mm.
