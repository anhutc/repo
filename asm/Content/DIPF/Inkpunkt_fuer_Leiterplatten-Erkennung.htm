Ink spot for PCB recognition

 

If two PCBs positioned immediately behind each other are assembled, this is known as a "combined PCB". During placement, both boards are treated as a single board. A special ink spot for PCB recognition is required in order to determine whether both boards are actually in the processing conveyor, since ink spot recognition is carried out before the fiducials are measured, which means that different tolerances have to be compensated for. For the same reason, the evaluation methods for the taught ink spot are also limited: Only the "mean grayscale" method with fixed parameters is available. The ink spot on the board must be at least 6 mm x 6 mm in size in order to compensate for any possible tolerances.

 

The ink spot type must be set in SIPLACE Pro.

A maximum of 1 ink spot for PCB recognition is permitted for each pair of boards.

The location of this ink spot must be specified in SIPLACE Pro.

This should be a clearly recognizable position at which the two adjoining boards that are to be assembled simultaneously are clearly different. If, for instance, the first board has a circular fiducial at the specified location and the second board has a via, this can lead to an error.

 

Recommendation: Specify a defined structure (e.g. a fiducial), i.e. a synthetic ink spot.
