Colinearity

If the positions of the ends of individual leads deviate from the typical positions of the ends of the leads of a group, it can be assumed that the lead concerned is bent. Bent leads are shown as being either too long or too short.

The leads whose ends deviate significantly are highlighted and an error message is issued.

 

This makes it possible to eliminate faulty components prior to placement.

 

You must specify the maximum tolerable deviation.

 

This additional inspection can increase the reject rate. It is therefore necessary to select the most appropriate tolerances possible.

 

Checking colinearity:

 

Groups with 3 or more leads A best fit line is calculated over the ends of the leads.
For each lead end, the system checks whether the distance from this line is less than or equal to the specified tolerance.
Group of 2 leads

A straight line is calculated that corresponds to the measured component angle and that lies between the two lead ends.

For both lead ends, the system checks whether the distance from this line is less than or equal to the specified tolerance.

 

Colinearity is only checked for leads of the type Gullwing (bent leads) or Wraparound (damaged leads) and for groups with more than one lead.

 

Example: One lead is bent in the left-hand group.
