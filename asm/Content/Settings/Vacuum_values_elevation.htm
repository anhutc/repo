Holding circuit vacuum values depending on height above sea level

If the machine is located more than 500m above sea level, it may be necessary to modify the parameters in the machine database.

Notes:

  • The vacuum in the holding circuit for the revolver heads should always be in the green area.

  • If revolver heads only are used, the vacuum tends towards the red line (2), and when revolver heads are mixed with C&P20 heads, it tends towards the green line (1)

Example:

During operation with revolver heads (red line, 2) approx. -600 mbar can be read in Munich at 500 meters above sea level.

The vacuum can always be read at the pump manometer (or at the measuring connection of the vacuum inlet block of the machine using an external manometer) and is passed virtually without loss to the heads if everything is airtight. Airtightness can be checked here with an acceptable level of accuracy.

During mixed operation (blie line, 1) on the other hand, there are no fixed values, as the different nozzle configurations mean that nozzles of different sizes are fitted to the C&P20 heads and varying degrees of leakage arise in the holding circuit. The vacuum at the pump then falls as low as 100 mbar and this lower vacuum is also passed without loss to the revolver heads. If the vacuum is read for mixed operation at 500 meters above sea level, a minimum of approx. -500 mbar is achieved.

A leakage test similar to that performed for the revolver heads can only be carried out in mixed operation if all the nozzles of all the C&P20 heads are sealed.

To perform a leakage test on the hoses on the machine, the hoses which lead to the heads can be withdrawn slightly and sealed with bungs.

In this case, the same values apply as for revolver heads (red line, 2).
