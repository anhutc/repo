Teach panels

The aim is to determine the position of the panel precisely enough to ensure that the placement positions on the panels also match the camera image.

All activities relate to the selected panel (indicated by a blue frame). The name of the panel is shown below the camera image.

Special case: Clusters

•   If the "reference panel" (row 1, column 1) of a cluster is selected, all the panels that belong to this cluster are also moved or rotated.

•   However, if you select any cluster panel and move it, the reference panel remains at the specified position. Only the offset between the rows and/or columns is changed.

X [mm]

Current X coordinate of the bottom left corner of the selected panel with reference to the superordinate panel.

Y [mm]

Current Y coordinate of the bottom left corner of the selected panel with reference to the superordinate panel.

Angle [°]

Current rotational offset of the panel relative to the superordinate panel.

Edit mode

The rotary controls and the rotary buttons are enabled, allowing you to change the position and rotational offset of the panel.

Rotary button

To perform fine adjustment of the rotational offset of a panel, press the mark on the rotary button and rotate it in the required direction with your finger.

It is not possible to rotate the panel if fiducials have been specified for it.

Rotary controls

For fine adjustment of the position of the panel.

You can roughly position the panel by pressing and moving it in the camera image.

Settings: Pseudocolors / high sensitivity / crosshair

+90°

The panel is rotated by 90° in an anticlockwise direction.

-90°

The panel is rotated by 90° in a clockwise direction.

 

The camera image for the next panel is displayed. You can move and rotate the model of this panel until it matches the camera image.

The previous panel is selected.

Caution: The previous/next panel is chosen according to the list in the table on the Details tab. Any sorting and selection that have been performed are taken into account.

Saving changes

Saves all the changes made since the last save operation.
