Calibrating the segment offset (method)

 

Note: The "Calibrate head segments" function is only used for the purposes of troubleshooting in the event that calibration of the head could not be carried out correctly. After troubleshooting and elimination of the errors, the head must be calibrated again completely. See Calibrate head.

  1. Make sure that nozzles of the correct type are fitted to all segments of the head: Type 1235 on the C&P20 head, type 2020 on the CPP head. Calibration is carried out using the segment currently in the placement/pickup position.
  2. Put the calibration tool into the calibration tool pocket keeping the printed (matt) side facing downward.
  3. Perform a reference run of all head and gantry axes. To do this, open the "Manual operations > Entire machine" view and press the Overall reference run button.
  4. Open the "Manual operations > Subsystems" view and press the appropriate button for the head you require, e.g. C&P20 head gantry 1 for the head on gantry 1.
  5. Press to switch to the "Head > Functions" view.
  6. Step the required segment to the placement/pickup position. Do this by pressing .
  7. Open the "Service" view and press the Single calibration button.
  8. In the "Service > Single calibration" view, press the Calibrate head button for the required head. The dialog box Service > Single calibration > Gantry n > Head opens.
  9. Choose Segments from the drop-down menu.
  10. Under Select segments, you can select all segments or individual segments for which calibration is to be performed.
  11. Press Execute single calibration. Calibration is started for the selected segments.
  12. After calibration has been completed successfully, press Accept to save the values determined during calibration in the machine data.
