Linear dipping unit 2 (LDU 2)

In the programming system it is possible to specify that components are to be dipped in flux before placement. This flux is provided using the linear dipping unit. The linear dipping unit is mounted on a component table in the same way as a feeder module.

The required flux is filled into the flux tank, and a squeegee operation makes it available in the depression (cavity) of the dipping plate.

During the squeegee operation, the squeegee unit is moved over the dipping plate with the flux tank hooked in place. The flux tank makes contact with the dipping plate and deposits a thin film of flux only in the region of the cavity. This is where components are dipped before placement. The surface of the flux is refreshed at regular intervals.

In its current version, the linear dipping unit (LDU 2) can be operated directly on the unit using a 2-line display and function keys as well as by the functions available at the GUI (Setup view and Manual operations) .
