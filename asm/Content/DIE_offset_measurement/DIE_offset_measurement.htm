Front/back offset measurement

With fan-out panel level packaging, silicon dies are picked up from a wafer and the die attach process is then used to place them on a larger panel with the electrical connections on top.

Inaccurate or oblique cut edges mean that determination of the position using the outline alone is inadequate for high-precision placement of dies. A new measurement step has therefore been introduced in which selected dies are measured both from the bottom and from the top.

The aim of front/back offset measurement is to be able to place the dies as precisely as possible, taking account of the electrical connections on the top surface.

The SIPLACE Wafer System or the wafer map can be used to specify which dies of a wafer are to be subject to this measurement. The average value for X and Y determined for these dies during additional measurement is used as the offset for all other dies of this wafer during placement.

A prerequisite for this is that a specified percentage of the measurements was able to be performed successfully. If the offset of a die is determined to be greater than the specified positional tolerance, the die is marked as an outlier and rejected. This measurement is not taken into account when determining the offset.

Front/back offset measurement process:

  • A die that has been selected for front/back offset measurement is picked up and measured with the component camera:
    The die is put down on the glass plate of the stationary component camera (SST 40) and measured from the bottom on the basis of its outline.

  • The result of this measurement is used to calculate the expected position of the top surface structure.

  • The board camera is moved above the center of the die as determined.

  • The precise position of the top surface structure is measured from above with the board camera (SST 34).

  • The offset between the expected position of the top surface structure and the measured position is determined.

  • After measurement has been completed, the die is placed on the panel, taking account of the offset determined for this die.

  • An average offset is then calculated across all successful front/back offset measurements.

Camera (SST 34) Board camera
Camera (SST 40) Stationary component camera with glass plate.

Note:

Before you start production, it is necessary to teach the components that are to be subject to this front/back offset measurement.
