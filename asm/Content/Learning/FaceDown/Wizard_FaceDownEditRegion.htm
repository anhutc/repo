Detection of flipped components using the brightness or contrast method: Specifying the region of interest

To allow the detection of flipped components, the mean brightness of a rectangle in the center of a component is determined. The result is given in brightness levels (range: 0 - 255).

Pale blue rectangle

Region of interest (ROI).
This region is suggested by the system and is placed in the center of the component being inspected.

 

X and Y position of the region of interest:


The region of interest is moved by one increment to the left, right, up or down.

Select the point in the image that you wish to define as the center of the pattern.

Length X and width Y of the region of interest:

The length or width is increased or decreased by 1 increment.

Step size

The value by which the specified parameter is incremented/decremented by clicking one of the buttons shown above.

The increment is increased or decreased.

You can also enter any of these values directly from the keyboard by clicking the relevant button and entering the required value using the numeric keypad.

 

Default region of interest

The region of interest is placed in the middle of the component at a default size.
