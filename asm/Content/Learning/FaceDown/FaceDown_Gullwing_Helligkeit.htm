Flipped components: Detection using gullwing leads

The Vision system determines the average brightness (possible values: 0 = dark through 255 = light) in a rectangle at the end of gullwing leads. The value measured from below is then compared with a brightness threshold (default: 127) to determine whether the component was picked up with the underside facing upward (i.e. flipped) or correctly.

If the component is flipped, the ends of the leads will generally be light.

 

Example:

Brightness threshold = 127

Flipped if brightness is above threshold: Yes

Correct:

     

The component was picked up and positioned in front of the camera correctly:

The region of interest marked in yellow on screen is obscured by the lead. The light does not reach the region of interest. It appears dark in the camera image.



The brightness value determined in the region of interest is lower than the specified brightness threshold.

 

Flipped:

   

 

The component was picked up and positioned in front of the camera upside down:

The region of interest is illuminated directly and appears light in the camera image.



The brightness value determined in the region of interest is greater than the specified brightness threshold.
