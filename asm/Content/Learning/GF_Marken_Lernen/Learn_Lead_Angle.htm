Detecting connections

The leads of the component are described in this view.

Number of learned connections

The total number of all connections (leads, balls, columns, features) that have already been recognized using the camera.

 

If you have selected a component shape for which several connection types are possible,
first select a connection type.

Leads:

All lead groups must be selected in sequence for detection. The following applies to a lead group: The leads must all be arranged in a row, must all be the same size and the distances between them must be the same.

  1. Where necessary, select the corresponding lead type.
    (Different lead types are only permitted for components of the component shape types connector, DPACK, nonstandard or socket).
  2. Now choose the direction in which the leads to be detected with the camera system are pointing in the camera image.
  3. In the camera image, click and hold down the left mouse button and drag to mark a region in which the lead group should be detected.
    The detected leads are highlighted in green after you have drag-selected the area.

Lead groups flagged with must be described according to the rules for the current type. The "Next" button is only enabled after detection of all groups has been completed.

Note:

It is not always possible for non-rectangular leads to be learned. The relevant data must then be entered in the component shape editor.

Always first select the direction and do not mark an area significantly larger than necessary.

Lead direction:

Leads which are arranged at a different angle to the component must be entered manually later.

If the component is rotated slightly, the angles of individual leads are often not determined exactly. They are, however, subsequently corrected automatically.

 

Other features:

Balls:

  1. Press Ball.
  2. In the camera image, drag-select the area in which the balls are to be detected.
    The detected balls are outlined in green after you have drag-selected the area. If balls are missing from the grid, yellow crosses are drawn at these positions.

Note: Step 1 is not required for components of the type BGA.

 

Irregularly arranged balls:

  1. Press Irregular ball groups.
  2. In the camera image, drag-select the area in which the balls are to be detected.
    The system attempts to learn all the balls in the marked area.
  3. Remove any artifacts that may be detected as features.

In the case of irregular group descriptions, the connection descriptions that are learned are neither checked nor corrected.

Column:

  1. Press Column.
  2. In the camera image, drag-select the area in which the columns are to be detected.
    The detected columns are outlined in green after you have drag-selected the area. If columns are missing from the grid, yellow crosses are drawn at these positions.

Note: Steps 1 and 2 are not performed for components of the type CCGA.

Irregularly arranged columns:

  1. Press Irregular column groups.
  2. In the camera image, drag-select the area in which the columns are to be detected.
    The system attempts to learn all the columns in the marked area.
  3. Remove any artifacts that may be detected as features.

In the case of irregular group descriptions, the connection descriptions that are learned are neither checked nor corrected.

Corners / Circular features:

  1. Select the most suitable corner shape or circle shape: Areas shaded gray correspond to a light representation in the camera image.
  2. In the camera image, drag-select the area in which the corner or circle is to be detected.
    The detected corner or hole is highlighted in green after you have drag-selected the area.

Note: When describing shields, the corners must be described; Drilled holes must not necessarily be specified.

 

Pattern:

  1. Press Pattern.
  2. In the camera image, drag-select the area in which the pattern is to be detected.
    The system attempts to learn all the selected edges in the marked area.

 

Delete pattern:

If features of the type Pattern have been described previously, specific regions of these can be explicitly deactivated for the purposes of detection.

  1. Press Delete pattern.
  2. In the camera image, drag-select the area in which no pattern is to be detected.
    All the contours already detected in this region are removed.
