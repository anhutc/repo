Pin1 detection (orientation mark)

Components which are rotated in the feeder cannot be measured and therefore also not be placed, as their actual orientation deviates too much from the expected orientation. Such components are rejected. When placing expensive components, this should, however, be avoided wherever possible.

If the arrangement of the connections makes it possible to identify the orientation, i.e. if the connections are arranged asymmetrically, the orientation detection inspection mode can be activated in order to identify whether a component is rotated.

If the connections are arranged symmetrically an additional orientation mark must be present on either the bottom side or the top side.

Pin1 detection on the basis of the orientation mark on the top side of the component cannot be performed until the top side of the component has been detected.

Components with 2 connections (e.g. tantalum)

Comparison of 2 regions:

The opposite sides are clearly different.
The region with the mark is designated as Pin1.

Components with more connections

Comparison of 4 regions:
The region that differs from the others in respect of shape or surface structure is designated as Pin1.

Advantage: It is not necessary to know the precise nature of the mark. It is not necessary to teach or describe any patterns.

 

For each component shape, you can set whether Pin1 detection is only to be used as an inspection criterion, i.e. components will be rejected if they are identified as rotated, or whether the results are used to calculate the placement angle (orientation value).

 

 

Start the wizard for Pin1 detection as follows:

  1. Switch to the view "List of measurement options" .

  2. Choose the measurement option Pin1 detection.

  3. Press Edit parameters.

 

The wizard is opened showing the Select illumination view.
