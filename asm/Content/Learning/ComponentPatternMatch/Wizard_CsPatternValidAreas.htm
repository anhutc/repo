Detection of the top side of the component: Regions of interest

To avoid increasing production times too much, it is useful to specify special regions that are compared during evaluation rather than measuring the entire component.

Recommendation:

  • You should always try to choose regions that have unique characteristics and clear transitions between light and dark.
  • With large components in particular, it is important to choose two opposite corners in order to exclude the possibility of the component being rotated.
  • Rows of leads should be avoided if possible, as evaluation can be ambiguous.

Currently selected area.

Green rectangle

Region of interest that has already been defined.

 

Add area

Manually define an area that is to be evaluated.

  1. Click Add area.
  2. A cross is shown in the image:
    Draw the region of interest by clicking in the window and then moving your finger over the touchscreen or moving the mouse pointer over the image while holding the left mouse button down (the position at which you pressed the left mouse button indicates the top left corner and the position at which you released the left mouse button indicates the bottom right corner).

Remove area

The currently selected area (shown in pale blue in the camera image and highlighted in color in the list) is deleted and will not be evaluated.
