Histogram method

The histogram indicates how often pixels of particular grayscale values occur in the search area.

 

The model comprises a histogram for the "go" case ("place") and a histogram for the "no-go" case ("do not place").

During classification, the histogram of the region of interest of the current image and the deviation from the two histograms of the model are determined.

The image is assigned to the class where there is less deviation from the histogram for that class.

If no assignment is possible, the machine is uncertain.

 

Advantage:

  • Very fast

  • No defined form required; classification is based on global properties of the image

  • Not sensitive to fluctuations in brightness

Disadvantage:

  • Position recognition must be carried out before ink spot recognition:
    You must ensure that the region of interest of the image is at the same position during classification as it was during teaching.
