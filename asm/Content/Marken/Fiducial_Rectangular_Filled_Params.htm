- Size tolerance X / Y

Extension of the fiducial in X and Y direction.

Corner radius

Describes the rounding of the corners:

The higher the value, the more rounded the corners will be.

The maximum value that can be set is half the length of the short sides.


Notch

Yes

Only one of the two shorter sides of a rectangular fiducial (in particular for oval holes) is relevant for the position of the fiducial.

Therefore, only the Y position is used to determine the position.

No

The X and Y positions are used to determine the position.

 

Oval holes as geometrical fiducials:

When describing oval holes, it is assumed that the corner radius has the maximum possible value. In other words, the two short sides describe semicircles.
