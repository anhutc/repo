Teaching an ink spot

The PCB camera takes images of ink spots during teaching. These images are used for ascertaining threshold values. Depending on whether the values for the current ink spot are above or below these threshold values, the ink spot is classified as an ink spot for placement or an ink spot for omitting. The ink spot cannot be definitively assigned if the ascertained value is in between the two threshold values. In this case, the operator has to decide what should be done with an ink spot that cannot be definitively identified.

Teaching must cover at least 1 ink spot for placement ("go") and 1 ink spot for omission ("no-go"). However, we recommend using 3 in order to achieve good results.

 

How to teach an ink spot:

  1. Open the Select fiducial view.
  2. Select a conveyor lane.
    All the fiducials and ink spots specified in the panel for this conveyor lane are shown in the fiducial list.
  3. Select a gantry.
  4. Select the corresponding entry from the fiducial list.
    Click directly on the entry or select the entry using the arrow keys on the keyboard.
  5. Place a board in the selected conveyor.
  6. Click the "Test and teach fiducial" icon.
    SIPLACE Vision is started and the board is moved to the relevant position.
  7. Click the "Geometry" icon .
  8. Click the Pattern button.
  9. Change the relevant parameters as necessary.
  10. Move the gantry manually so that the pattern is shown in the center of the camera image.
  11. Mark the size of the area in which the system is to search for the ink spot:
    ROI (Region of Interest)    . After you have defined the size of the ROI, the search area is placed in the center of the camera image.
    If you change the region of interest during teaching, this change also applies to all images taught previously.
  12. If the ink spot identifies a panel which is to be assembled ("go" case), click the Place button, and if it identifies a panel which is not to be assembled ("no-go" case), click the Do not place button.
    The ink spot model generated in this way is stored in the representation under "Previous images".
  13. Repeat the teaching process (5 - 11) with a different panel or PCB to describe the other case (place / do not place).
    At least one pattern must be stored for each of these cases.
  14. You finish teaching an ink spot by clicking the Automatic.
    Ink spot measurements are carried out in automatic mode.
