SMPS - Switched Modular Power Supply

The following AC/DC converters are used:

    -   QT40-999.70: Generates the 300 V and 160 V DC operating voltage

    -   QT40-241: Generates 22-28 V DC voltages

    -   QT40-361: Generates 34-42 V DC voltages

The internal DC/DC converter (24 V) QT20-241 cannot be polled by the diagnostics function.

CAP (capacitor bank)

Serial no.

Capacitor bank identifier (label).

Ambient temperature

Temperature inside the capacitor bank.

Current output voltage (160 V)

The current output voltage supplied to the head. Expected: 160 V.

Current output voltage (300 V)

The current output voltage supplied to the gantries. Expected: 300 V.

Buffer capacitance

If this value drops below 80 % of the nominal value, the value is highlighted and a warning is issued. The capacitor bank should be replaced.

Total operating hours

Total number of hours the capacity bank has been in operation to date.

AC/DC converter (QT40-999.70, QT40-241, QT40-361)

Serial no.

Corresponds to the identifier on the AC/DC converter (label).

Input voltage

Current input voltage from the customer's power supply network.

Temperature

Current ambient temperature - external temperature of the AC/DC converter.

Maximum

Maximum recorded ambient temperature.

Parameter

Operating hours since start

Number of hours the power supply has been switched on since the machine was last started.

Total operating hours

Total number of hours the AC/DC converter has been switched on for.

External voltage peaks

Number of voltage spikes from the customer's power supply network.

Over temperature shutdowns

Number of times the AC/DC converter has shut down due to overtemperature.

Over input voltage shutdowns

Number of times the AC/DC converter has shut down due to overvoltage from the customer's power supply network.

Input under voltage drops

Number of times the AC/DC converter has shut down due to undervoltage from the customer's power supply network.

Highest input voltage

The highest value to date for the input voltage from the customer's power supply network in [V].
