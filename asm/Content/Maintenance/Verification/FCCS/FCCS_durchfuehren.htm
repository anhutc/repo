Performing camera verification

In order to carry out illumination calibration, a suitable calibration tool must be positioned in front of each stationary camera or head camera. To do this, the calibration tool is picked up with a special nozzle and positioned in front of the camera.

The segment to be used for this can be freely selected. In the case of a stationary camera, the head to be used can also be selected freely as required.

Prerequisites for performing illumination calibration:

  • The machine must have been referenced and calibrated.

  • The middle lane must have been removed on machines with 3 lanes.

  • The glass plate must be removed in the case of type SST 47 cameras.

 

Proceed as follows to perform an illumination calibration:

  1. Ensure that there are no more boards in the machine and that there are no more components on the heads.

  2. Switch to the view Maintenance -> Verification and press Camera verification .

  3. Place the camera verification plate in the input conveyor.

  4. Select the cameras whose brightness is to be calibrated.
    If no suitable nozzle is available for a camera, this must first be made available on the head or in the nozzle changer.

  5. Press the Next button.

  6. Ensure that all the prerequisites displayed are met.

  7. Press Start.

 

Caution:

You must evaluate the calibration values returned yourself, in order to carry out further investigations on the relevant camera before actually saving the results, for instance, if there are significant discrepancies.
