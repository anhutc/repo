Nozzle scanning: Determined soiling

The aim is to reliably identify any soiling present on the nozzle.

Bright, shiny areas indicate soiling (e.g. with solder paste).

Areas with a red border

Soiling detected.

Areas with an orange border

Although the camera image shows a bright area that can be interpreted as soiling from solder, the soiling indicated is smaller than the upper limit, so the soiling is not detected by nozzle scanning.

 

Tip:

Increase the sensitivity for this nozzle type in the programming system.

 

Soiling

Indicates the percentage of the front face that has been identified as soiled.

Upper limit

If the specified level of soiling is above this limit, the nozzle is identified as "soiled" and must be cleaned or replaced.

The limit is calculated in the programming system on the basis of the sensitivity setting: The higher the sensitivity setting, the lower the upper limit. I.e. smaller bright areas are identified as soiling.

Sensitivity level

Specified by the programming system:

Sensitivity of the nozzle inspection. Changing the sensitivity should ensure that particularly bright soiling (solder) is also detected reliably.

This setting only takes effect as of level 2 of nozzle scanning (checking the nozzle for soiling):

Low (default)

Low sensitivity inspection.

 

Medium

Medium sensitivity inspection.

A second image is captured with different illumination for these two settings.

Caution: This can lead to a loss of placement performance.

High

High sensitivity inspection.

 

Possible error message:

841

Nozzle dirty

Clean or replace the nozzle.

The machine is stopped if "Stop machine" is set for the scanning level "Check for soiling".
