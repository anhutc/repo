Example: Adjusting the exposure time

 

In the case of a component with extremely reflective connections, it is possible for virtually all the light to be reflected. It then becomes impossible for the camera to "see" certain areas of the connection. This is represented by black spots in the topographical image.

 

Example: BGA

Good data quality:

Significant areas with no data points (black spots).

1  Top of the ball well represented

2  Reflections from the neighboring ball

1  The black spots indicate missing data points.
   

 

This can be partially compensated for by increasing the exposure time. However, increasing this value can also result in increased interference reflections.

 

Caution: Increasing the exposure time results in an increased scan time.
