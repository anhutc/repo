Recognition step: Solder heights: Differences from model

Shows the differences between the current and the learned average height of a solder deposit.

  • The average height is calculated for each solder deposit in the model and on the current board.

  • These two heights are compared and the difference is shown in the diagram:
    A column is shown for each solder deposit where the current average height differs from the average height in the model. The base of the column corresponds to the area of the solder deposit and the height of the column corresponds to the height difference.

  • Depending on the setting under "Average solder height as reference", the height difference refers to the corresponding solder deposit in the model or to the calculated average of all solder deposits in the model.

  • A yellow rectangle is shown at the distance given under "Maximum height difference for solder deposits". If the difference exceeds this value, the solder deposit is faulty.
