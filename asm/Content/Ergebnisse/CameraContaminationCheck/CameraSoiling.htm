Checking the camera for soiling

This inspection is intended to determine whether a camera is dirty and whether camera verification is necessary.

A contrast tool (standard contrast) with light and dark areas is used. This is placed in the calibration tool pocket of the machine instead of the calibration tool. During inspection, the contrast tool is automatically picked up from the calibration tool pocket and an image of it is captured using the camera that is to be tested.

Because the machines have two different size calibration tool pockets, two different size contrast tools are also required.

- Small contrast tool (identical with the small calibration tool): 7.5 mm x 7.5 mm x 1 mm

- Large contrast tool (identical with the large calibration tool): 14 mm x 14 mm x 1 mm

The inspection returns the degree to which the camera is soiled and the extent to which it is necessary to inspect the camera (brightness calibration).

  • If the mean brightness in the dark areas exceeds a specified threshold, the camera is dirty and must be cleaned.

  • If the mean brightness in the light areas falls too much, brightness calibration must be carried out on the camera.
