Determining the position of substrates in the machine

When placing components on substrates (wafers, carriers), it is first necessary to determine the precise position and orientation of the substrate in the machine. Because substrates generally have no position fiducials, the position and orientation are determined on the basis of the outer edge.

The substrate is measured in the machine using the board camera:

1.

Determine the X/Y position of the substrate:

 

 

 

The position of the substrate can be determined with the board camera on the basis of at least 3 measurement points along the outer edge of the substrate.

 

 

 

The measurement points are as far from each other as possible. E.g. if 3 measurement points are used, they are 120° from each other and if 4 measurement points are used, they are 90° from each other.

 

 

2.

Determine the orientation of the substrate (notch detection):

 

 

 

A notch on the outer edge determines the orientation of the substrate in the machine.

 

 

 

Prerequisites for notch detection:

 

 

 

- The geometry of the notch has been described.

 

 

 

- Notch detection has been enabled.

 

 

 

If no notch has been specified for the substrate, the angle is always 0°.

 

 

 

As a rule, it is assumed that the substrate is dark and is lying on a lighter carrier (bright metal).
