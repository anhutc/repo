Cracked die inspection

In the programming system, you can specify whether dies are to be inspected for damage (cracks) in the tape or tray before they are picked up. If a faulty die is detected, it remains in the tape or tray.

To do this, an image of the upper side of the dies is captured with the board camera using steep illumination.

The dies are to be inspected:

  • The first n dies when production starts and each time the feeder is changed.

  • Verification interval: You can specify the number of dies after which the measurement is to be repeated.

You can also specify whether the feeder is to be deactivated after one or more damaged dies are detected.

The following scenarios are detected:

The two broken parts are still connected to each other, but are creased:

The two parts are shown at different brightnesses with the board camera.

Die is completely fractured:

The two broken parts are shown with similar brightnesses, but with a dark gap at the fracture.

The following prerequisites apply if a fracture is to be detected on the basis of the top surface of the die:

•

The following applies along the edge of the fracture (at least 85%):

 
- The difference in grayscale values between the broken parts is at least 15 grayscale increments.
  Alternatively:
- The dark gap between the two broken parts is at least 2 pixels wide.

•

The smaller broken part represents at least 5 % of the surface area of the die.

•

If the edge of the fracture is not straight, the curvature must not exceed 0.1 mm or 5% of the size of the die.
