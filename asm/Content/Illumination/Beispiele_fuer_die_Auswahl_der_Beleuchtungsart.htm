Examples for the selection of the illumination type

With the PCB camera, the individual levels are characterized less by the illumination angle than by the wavelength of the illumination source. With the camera currently used, you can select between white, blue or infrared (IR) illumination. The illumination color that will deliver the best results depends on the material of both the fiducial and of the board substrate. The objective is to maximize the contrast between the fiducial and the substrate. Sometimes, it is sensible to suppress artifacts in the region of the fiducial at the same time. The wide range of variables means that it is difficult to offer general guidelines. The following recommendations can therefore only be seen as a starting point; on occasion, it may be necessary to make different settings.

• Bright or tinned fiducials on a dark substrate (often green or brown): In this case, white or IR light deliver good results.

• Fiducials with solder resist: IR light is often absorbed least by the resist, which is why the greatest contrast is often achieved with IR illumination.

• Fiducials on a ceramic substrate: Blue illumination is often appropriate here. In this case, the fiducials appear as a dark structure on a light substrate.
