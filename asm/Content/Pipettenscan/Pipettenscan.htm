Nozzle scanning

Nozzle scanning is understood to be the analysis of the suction ducts and front face of nozzles by detecting brightness structures.

Level 1: Determination of the nozzle position and orientation

It is only possible to carry out the following tests successfully if the system can accurately detect the position and orientation of a nozzle in front of the camera.

Level 2: Checking the nozzle for soiling

Soiling caused by solder paste or metal abrasion, for instance, and which increases the brightness of the front face so much that it can influence measurement of a component is detected.

Level 3: Checking the suction surface for soiling

Soiling or blockage of the suction ducts can no longer be detected with a vacuum check in the case of ducts with small cross-sections.

Level 4: Examination of the end surface for damage

The brightness distribution on the end surface can indicate damage to the surface.

 

You can enter the individual levels at the station GUI (Settings view > Machine options).

Position cannot be determined

841

Nozzle dirty

The machine is stopped if "Stop machine" is set for the scanning level "Check for soiling".

842

Suction duct blocked

Clean or replace the nozzle.

Warning

Nozzle possibly worn

844

Nozzle scan unsuccessful

Warning

847

Socket dirty
