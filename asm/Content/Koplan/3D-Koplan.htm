3D coplanarity sensor

The 3D sensor is an optical inspection system:

This has the job of determining the offsets of the features (leads, balls) of a component with respect to the placement plane (coplanarity) and the angle to the perpendicular (colinearity) and compare this with a specified tolerance (derived from the thickness of the solder paste).

A valid evaluation makes a statement as to whether all the measured lead discrepancies are within the specified tolerances.

 

Measurement can be carried out with the 3D sensor for some component types. The specification as to whether it is possible to carry out such a measurement is made in the programming system.

With BGA components, the height of the individual balls is determined and then checked to ensure that the measured heights meet the coplanarity requirements.

With components with gull wing leads (without notch), the position of the individual placement planes is determined and checked to ensure that the discrepancies between the measured lead heights are within the specified tolerances.
